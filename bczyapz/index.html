The thesis

Most podcasts are voice files in a wrapper. The conversation happened, the file lives somewhere, and the knowledge inside is locked behind an hour of listening. BCZ YapZ treats every conversation as a piece of public infrastructure - the audio is one surface, but the transcript, the show notes, the quotes, and the agent-readable context all ship with the episode.

Pair that with the ZAO ecosystem on Farcaster, and you get an evolving public record of what builders, artists, and operators are actually working on - in their own words, dated, searchable, transcribable, and remixable.

How BCZ YapZ is different

Format

Long-form, low-edit

Real conversations. No interruption-marketing, no awkward sponsorship reads mid-thought. If it ran an hour, you get an hour.

Distribution

Multi-surface, RSS-first

YouTube for video. RSS for podcast apps. Farcaster for clips + discussion. Transcripts on the site for search + agents.

Context

Agent-readable archive

Every episode page exposes structured metadata so an LLM can search "what did Cassie say about Hypersnap auth?" and find the right minute.

Guests

Ecosystem-aligned

Builders shipping in public, artists building owned audiences, operators inside the ZAO + Farcaster ecosystem. Not influencer-cycle bookings.
